# gallery-tier-taxonomy

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Gallery Targeter (Atelier §3.5) and into the Dossier writer when AKB.representation fields populate. Gallery representation is a tiered system; artists who submit to the wrong tier waste the one cold-pitch opportunity they have with a given gallery. The rule: tier is determined by *who they represent*, not by square footage or social-media follower count. Top-tier galleries are closed to unsolicited submissions; representation at that level happens through curator referral, museum-show cascades, and poached representations from tier-down galleries. Regional and emerging galleries often publicize open-call windows that produce real CV lines. This file defines the tiers, names representative examples, and states the entry pathway for each.

---

## 1. Tier A — Blue-chip primary representation (top global galleries)

These galleries set auction-market expectations for their artists and underwrite museum-scale production. They do not accept unsolicited submissions.

- **Gagosian** — 19 locations globally; represents Richard Prince, Cy Twombly estate, Jeff Koons, Andreas Gursky, Sally Mann (photography) ([Gagosian](https://gagosian.com/)).
- **Hauser & Wirth** — represents Louise Bourgeois estate, Mark Bradford, Roni Horn, Rashid Johnson ([Hauser & Wirth](https://www.hauserwirth.com/)).
- **David Zwirner** — represents Luc Tuymans, Wolfgang Tillmans, Diane Arbus estate, Roy DeCarava estate ([David Zwirner](https://www.davidzwirner.com/)).
- **Pace Gallery** — represents Richard Misrach, Elizabeth Murray estate, Irving Penn Foundation, Robert Rauschenberg ([Pace](https://www.pacegallery.com/)).
- **White Cube** — London, Paris, Hong Kong, Seoul, NYC; represents Chuck Close estate, Andreas Gursky (shared), Tracey Emin ([White Cube](https://www.whitecube.com/)).
- **Thaddaeus Ropac** — Paris, London, Salzburg, Seoul; represents Robert Mapplethorpe Foundation, Robert Rauschenberg ([Thaddaeus Ropac](https://ropac.net/)).

**Entry pathway.** None via submission. Artists arrive here through (a) poached representation from a Tier B primary gallery after a breakout museum show, (b) curator-director introductions following a biennial inclusion (Whitney Biennial, Documenta, Venice), (c) estate management inheritance. Cold-pitching a Tier A gallery is an anti-signal — it marks the artist as misreading the market.

**Signals that matter here.** Museum solo show, biennial inclusion, institutional collection across three or more top-50 museums, auction record.

## 2. Tier B — Established primary galleries

These galleries represent mid-to-late-career artists at sustained mid-six-to-seven-figure prices, run booths at Frieze / Art Basel / Armory, and frequently place work in museum collections.

- **Marian Goodman** — represents Jeff Wall, Dan Graham, Tacita Dean ([Marian Goodman](https://www.mariangoodman.com/)).
- **303 Gallery** — represents Collier Schorr, Doug Aitken, Stephen Shore (shared history) ([303 Gallery](https://www.303gallery.com/)).
- **Petzel** — NYC; represents Seth Price, Pamela Rosenkranz ([Petzel](https://www.petzel.com/)).
- **Andrew Kreps** — NYC; represents Roe Ethridge, Ricci Albenda ([Andrew Kreps](https://www.andrewkreps.com/)).
- **Karma** — NYC; represents Mathew Wong estate, Nicolas Party ([Karma](https://karmakarma.org/)).
- **Miguel Abreu** — NYC; represents Jean-Luc Moulène, Eileen Quinlan ([Miguel Abreu](https://miguelabreugallery.com/)).

**Entry pathway.** Tier B galleries watch Tier C mid-career programs, artist-run spaces, and biennials. They accept artists through (a) curator or critic referral, (b) museum-show visibility, (c) studio visits arranged by trusted Tier C dealers. Formal submissions are almost always ignored.

---

## 3. Tier C — Mid-career primary galleries

Smaller footprint, sharp programs, often single-city operations but with a clear international secondary-market pathway. These are the galleries that Tier B galleries watch for breakout artists.

- **Foxy Production** — Brooklyn; represents Sara Greenberger Rafferty ([Foxy Production](https://www.foxyproduction.com/)).
- **Bureau** — NYC LES; represents Erica Baum, Sarah Crowner ([Bureau](https://www.bureau-inc.com/)).
- **Bridget Donahue** — NYC; represents Susan Cianciolo, Lisa Alvarado ([Bridget Donahue](https://bridgetdonahue.nyc/)).
- **JTT** — NYC; represents Jamian Juliano-Villani (before her shift to Gagosian) and others ([JTT](https://jttnyc.com/)).
- **Foley Gallery** — NYC LES. No longer accepting unsolicited submissions but runs the **Exhibition Lab** — a cohort-based career development program photographers can enter as a pathway toward gallery visibility ([Foley Gallery Details](http://www.foleygallery.com/details)).

**Entry pathway.** Via **referral** — from another gallery, a critic, a curator, or an artist already in the roster. A few accept occasional studio-visit requests from artists with strong exhibition CVs; most do not. The Exhibition Lab at Foley is the canonical example of a Tier-C gallery running a paid cohort program as its unsolicited-submission replacement.

## 4. Tier D — Regional flagship galleries

These galleries are *the* best gallery in their region for a specific medium or scale. They produce the representation most working mid-career artists realistically target.

- **Edwynn Houk Gallery** (NYC, photography-specialist since 1977) — represents Abelardo Morell, Matthew Pillsbury, Sally Mann (since 1989), Robert Polidori, Stephen Shore, Annie Leibovitz. Seven-artist contemporary roster plus estate representation (Robert Frank, Charles Sheeler, Lillian Bassman) ([Edwynn Houk Gallery](https://www.houkgallery.com/gallery/); [Houk Artists page](https://www.houkgallery.com/artists/)). No public submission process.
- **Yossi Milo** (NYC, photography) — represents Matthew Brandt, Pieter Hugo, Sze Tsung Nicolás Leong. Submissions explicitly closed: "Yossi Milo is currently not accepting artists' submissions and cannot be held responsible for any unsolicited materials shared with the gallery" ([Yossi Milo about](https://yossimilo.com/about/)).
- **Dowling Walsh** (Rockland, Maine) — represents Alex Katz (alongside Gladstone), Jamie Wyeth, Lois Dodd. Regional flagship for Maine contemporary painting and photography ([Dowling Walsh](https://dowlingwalsh.com/)).
- **Paulson Fontaine Press** (Berkeley) — print and multiples publisher with gallery programming.
- **Stephen Bulger Gallery** (Toronto, photography) — represents Larry Towell, Lynne Cohen estate ([Stephen Bulger](https://bulgergallery.com/)).

**Entry pathway.** By referral or curator introduction; occasional studio visits via AIPAD (Association of International Photography Art Dealers) network for photography galleries specifically ([AIPAD](https://www.aipad.com/)). Some regional galleries accept digital portfolio submissions once or twice a year — verify current policy on the gallery's site before sending.

**Signals.** Inclusion in regional museum biennial, state-council fellowship, book publication with a recognized photography press, prior Tier E solo shows.

---

## 5. Tier E — Emerging / open-submission galleries and artist-run spaces

Where a mid-career artist without gallery representation actually gets a first show. Many of these programs are explicitly open to unsolicited applicants and produce exhibition CV lines.

- **Foley Gallery Exhibition Lab.** Paid cohort program — photographers develop a portfolio through structured review with the opportunity for exhibition at Foley ([Foley Gallery](http://www.foleygallery.com/)).
- **Fastnet** (Cape Cod / NYC) — emerging photography open-submission exhibitions.
- **P·P·O·W** (NYC) — established gallery, occasional open submissions for project-based exhibitions ([PPOW](https://www.ppowgallery.com/)).
- **Artist-run spaces.** Cue Art Foundation (NYC) open-call curator-mentorship program, ABC No Rio, Cooper Cole (Toronto), Good Weather (Little Rock), Tops (Memphis).
- **Regional museum-affiliated exhibition spaces.** University galleries, state-arts-council commissioned exhibitions, Kohler Arts Center (Sheboygan), John Michael Kohler Arts/Industry residency.

**Entry pathway.** Open-submission window (commonly fall or spring); curator-mentored application ([Cue Art Foundation](https://cueartfoundation.org/)); artist-juried selection for group show.

## 6. What the Targeter must do with this file

For a given applicant with known career-stage markers:

1. Locate the applicant's current tier (no representation → Tier E; regional emerging representation → Tier D; represented in one major metro → Tier C; biennial-included → Tier B pipeline).
2. Target *one tier up*, not three tiers up. A Tier E artist cold-pitching a Tier A gallery signals a misread of the system; a Tier E artist pitching Tier D with a well-curated 10-image body, exhibition history, and fellowship CV is responding to the actual market.
3. Before naming a specific gallery target, verify that gallery's current submission policy on their own website. Policies shift: galleries that accepted submissions in 2022 may not in 2026, and vice versa.
4. Match the gallery's existing roster by medium and register. A landscape-forward photographer cold-pitching a gallery whose roster is conceptual video work is a bad fit regardless of tier match.

---

## 7. Dossier copy language by tier

- **Tier A / B target.** "Not reachable by cold submission at current career stage; museum-show cascade is the only path. Prioritize biennial inclusion and institutional collection."
- **Tier C target.** "Requires curator or critic referral. Identify three potential referrers from the applicant's existing network (past jurors, residency directors, prior gallery contacts)."
- **Tier D target.** "Verify submission policy; most accept introductions via AIPAD network or past fellowship jurors."
- **Tier E target.** "Open-call window deadlines listed in opportunity-sources.md; realistic next exhibition pathway."

The dossier must name the tier explicitly. "Target gallery: [name], Tier D regional-flagship photography specialist." Not "consider reaching out to galleries." Vagueness here defeats the purpose of the Gallery Targeter.
