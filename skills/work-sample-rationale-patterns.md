# work-sample-rationale-patterns

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Package Drafter for the `work_sample_selection` material (rationale text that accompanies each image in an application portfolio), and consulted when generating the `additional details` captions that MacDowell, Yaddo, and most residency SlideRoom portals attach to each uploaded work sample. Specifies a three-sentence formula per image, the sentence-level register, per-institution caption-length conventions, and four worked examples. The Drafter reads one work sample at a time, loads the corresponding portfolio-image EXIF + Style Analyst tags, and produces rationale text following the formula.

---

## 1. The three-sentence formula

Every image rationale lands three sentences, in this order, each doing a specific job:

1. **What the image IS.** Subject, date, dimensions, process. Concrete. "Kennebec River at ice breakup, March 2023; 4x5 color negative, archival pigment print on Hahnemühle Photo Rag, 40x50 inches, edition of 5." This sentence is often comma-delimited rather than grammatical — the goal is data density, not prose.
2. **What the image DOES formally.** The composition, light, palette, and one formal decision load-bearing on meaning. "Centered axial horizon at the exact hour of thaw; a slate-rust-lichen palette compressed by the overcast; the ice wedge in the foreground operates as repoussoir without sentimentality."
3. **Why THIS institution will respond.** Cohort-fit, thematic fit, or process alignment grounded in a specific recent recipient of this institution. "The serial-return structure sits adjacent to the durational practice the Foundation's 2024 cohort rewarded (Krajnak, Brandt)."

Optional fourth sentence when the image is doing something sample-specific (one of two details of the same work, a progress-state image, an installation view): name what role this image plays in the set. "Installation view; scale reference for the 24-print sequence at Dowling Walsh."

Total length per image caption: 50–90 words, 3–4 sentences. Exceeding 90 words routinely cuts against reader attention; below 30 words is thin.

## 2. Per-institution caption conventions

- **MacDowell.** The `additional details` box is unlimited but panels read 8 captions per applicant; compression is rewarded. Target 50–70 words per image. Detail captions (2 of the 8 allowed as detail / installation views) should explicitly state "detail" or "installation view" in sentence one ([MacDowell Work Sample Requirements](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/work-sample-requirements)).
- **Yaddo.** 7 images; no prescribed caption format. Yaddo's admissions committee views images on personal computer screens and via projection ([Yaddo Work Sample Requirements](https://yaddo.org/work-sample-requirements/)); captions of 50–80 words read well in both contexts.
- **Guggenheim.** The *List of Works* (up to 10 items, visual artists) is not a caption set — it's a spreadsheet-like list of title, year, medium, dimensions, venue / collection. The rationale in this file applies to the *work samples* which are uploaded separately as images; caption length should match the List of Works in factual density but add the second and third formula sentences the List of Works does not include ([Guggenheim Application Handbook, 2026](https://competition.gf.org/assets/Guide-to-the-JSGMF-2026.pdf)).
- **Creative Capital.** Round I uses one work sample; Round II expands to several. Round I's caption is the single highest-leverage caption in the application and should carry a full three-sentence formula plus one additional sentence tying the image to the proposed project ([Creative Capital Grant Application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)).
- **Joan Mitchell Fellowship.** 10 work samples through Submittable; files labeled `LastName_firstinitial_1-10.[ext]` ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship Application Guidelines 2025](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/artist-programs/joan-mitchell-fellowship-application-guidelines-2025)). Standard 60–80 word captions work.
- **Pollock-Krasner.** 10 digital images with a "corresponding identification list" ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/)). The identification list is a compact image-by-image document: title, year, medium, dimensions — the three-sentence rationale is better placed in the artist statement or cover letter than in the ID list.

---

## 3. Sentence-level register rules

- **Sentence one is comma-spliced data, not prose.** Resist the urge to "write" sentence one. "Kennebec River at ice breakup, March 2023; 4x5 color negative, archival pigment print, 40x50 inches, edition of 5" reads faster than "This is a photograph taken on the Kennebec River during the ice breakup in March 2023, using a 4x5 color negative format."
- **Sentence two uses formal vocabulary from `aesthetic-vocabulary.md`.** Name the composition, the light, the palette in that file's terms. Do not invent descriptive phrases.
- **Sentence three names a specific cohort member or body of work at the target institution.** This sentence is institution-specific; the same image should get three different sentence-threes if applying to three different programs. The Drafter reads the Rubric Matcher's cohort-analysis output for the target opportunity to source the named precedent.
- **No adjectives of effect in sentences two and three.** *Beautiful, striking, powerful, dramatic, haunting* all disqualify the rationale from reading as institutional.

## 4. Worked examples

### Example A — photography, landscape, Guggenheim Fellowship

> *Kennebec River at Hallowell, Thaw #3*, March 2023. 4x5 color negative, archival pigment on Hahnemühle Photo Rag, 40x50 inches, edition of 5. Centered axial horizon at the exact hour of ice movement; slate-rust-lichen palette compressed by the overcast; an ice wedge in the foreground operates as repoussoir without sentiment. The durational-return structure and material-indexing sits in the conversation the 2024 photography cohort's Brandt and Krajnak selections define, and extends that cohort's range into a northeastern-watershed subject not yet represented.

### Example B — photography, detail view, MacDowell

> *Androscoggin Ice, Detail #2* (detail), 2023. 8x10 silver gelatin contact print on Ilford Galerie, 8x10 inches, unique. Detail of the second plate from the same day as Image 5; shows the chemical wash residue left by the freezing river water on the film edge, which is retained as subject matter. Included to convey the material-indexing register the project depends on, which is not visible at full-frame scale in Image 5.

### Example C — painting, Pollock-Krasner

> *Afterimage VII*, 2024. Oil on un-stretched linen, 60 x 48 inches, hung from the top edge with a one-inch reveal. Lead-tin yellow under-ground worked wet-into-wet in a single session per the alla-prima procedure, finished with a scumbled grey that lifts the yellow in broken patches at the figure's shoulder line. The scale and figure-ground register extend the working vocabulary established in Marlene Dumas's *Great Men* portraits, and the single-session method derives from Luc Tuymans's working practice ([Apollo Magazine interview](https://apollo-magazine.com/a-necessary-realism-interview-with-luc-tuymans/)).

### Example D — sculpture, Joan Mitchell Fellowship

> *Kennebec Drift II*, 2023. Cast iron from local green-sand molds, ferric-nitrate patina, steel rod armature. 22 x 18 x 14 inches, 47 lbs. Hand-built molds taken from driftwood collected at the river's mouth; the casting retains the wood's bark texture as surface, read as truth-to-materials in the Brâncuși lineage rather than the Serra architectural-scale register. The process-forward casting method and the regional-material sourcing sit adjacent to the cast-material practice the 2023 Joan Mitchell Fellowship recognized in multiple recipients (see Fellowship cohort notes in the Rubric Matcher output for this opportunity).

### Example E — video, Creative Capital

> *Thaw Loop*, 2024. Single-channel HD video, 1920 x 1080, 16:9, 24fps, stereo audio, TRT 9:13, installed as projection at 12 feet wide. Fixed-tripod frame on the Kennebec at the ice-breakup point, 27 overlapping takes recorded between 6:15 and 9:48 a.m. on March 22, 2023, sequenced as an accelerating loop that collapses 3 hours into 9 minutes. The held-frame durational register and compression-editing method extend from Stan Douglas's looped cinema-register work and Hito Steyerl's essay-video construction, in a climate-witness register the Foundation's 2023 panel identified as a priority ([Creative Capital 2027 Application Handbook](https://creative-capital.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/2027-Creative-Capital-Application-Handbook.pdf)).

## 5. Failure modes

- **Plot summary instead of formal description.** "This image shows the river in winter, with ice forming along the banks." Tells the juror what they can already see. The rationale tells the juror *what they cannot see at first glance*: why this image was selected from the 8,000 frames the body of work produced, and what it does formally.
- **Generic cohort-fit claim.** "This work aligns with the Foundation's mission" is a fail. Sentence three must name a specific recipient or body of work.
- **Over-technical sentence one.** Camera body, lens, ISO, shutter speed are below the institutional register — MacDowell does not need to know the artist shot at f/11 at 1/60. Keep sentence one to medium, format, print specs, dimensions, edition.
- **Caption bloat.** A 180-word rationale on a 50-word image reads as over-explanation. Trim.
- **Re-use across institutions without re-targeting sentence three.** The same image applying to MacDowell, Guggenheim, and Pollock-Krasner gets three different third sentences — the first two sentences can be stable. A drafter that re-uses all three sentences across institutions is losing the cohort-fit leverage the rationale exists to deliver.

---

## 6. Edge case: when the rationale should be shorter or absent

- **Pollock-Krasner identification list.** Use two-sentence rationale at most; the cover letter carries the argument. ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/))
- **Gallery submissions and museum donation pitches.** The cover letter carries the cohort-fit argument; image captions stay to sentences one and two only, institutional data with no argumentative sentence three.
- **Open-call competitions with no caption field.** Captions collapse to a filename convention: `LastName_Title_Year_Dimensions.jpg`. The Drafter should detect this case from the opportunity's submission-format metadata and skip rationale generation.
