# submission-calendar

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Orchestrator system prompt for window-aware ranking, and into the Dossier writer for "what's in the next 30 / 60 / 90 days" synthesis. Opportunity deadlines cluster into a narrow set of seasonal patterns across the calendar; ranking a portfolio's pipeline without respecting those clusters produces dossier copy that lists long-dead windows next to windows that just opened. This file is the canonical month-by-month index. Cite primary application pages when the ranking narrative names a specific program; do not invent dates.

---

## 1. Month-by-month index

Each bullet lists programs with an *active application window* (open-to-deadline) that overlaps the month. A program whose deadline falls on the first of a month is listed in the month the window *closes*, not the month it opens.

**January.** Ucross Fall residency deadline (Jan 15, for Aug–Dec residencies) ([Ucross Submission Manager](https://ucrossfoundation.submittable.com/submit)). Creative Capital open-call window typically runs Dec into early April for the biennial Visual Arts cycle; January is the mid-window month ([Creative Capital Award application page](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)). MacDowell Fall/Winter cycle window open (Jan 15 open, Feb 10 deadline) ([MacDowell Apply page](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/apply-for-fellowship)). Yaddo Winter deadline (Dec 20) has just closed; no Yaddo window active.

**February.** NEA Grants for Arts Projects, Cycle 1: Grants.gov Part 1 deadline Feb 12, NEA Applicant Portal Part 2 deadline Feb 25 ([NEA Grants for Arts Projects](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects)). MacDowell Fall/Winter deadline Feb 10 ([MacDowell Application Guidelines](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/application-guidelines)). Creative Capital open call still active. Vermont Studio Center rolling review feeds the June 30 / September 30 deadlines ahead ([Vermont Studio Center Apply](https://vermontstudiocenter.org/apply)).

**March.** Vermont Studio Center Spring deadline March 31 for subsequent-season residencies ([VSC press release](https://vermontstudiocenter.org/press-room/2025/8/14/vermont-studio-center-welcomes-applications-for-summer-and-fall-2026-residencies)). Creative Capital final window (deadline usually early April). Ucross Native American Fellowship and other supplementary programs ([Ucross Native American Fellowship](https://www.ucrossfoundation.org/native-american-fellowships.html)).

**April.** Creative Capital deadline (early April, ~3pm ET on submission day) ([Creative Capital press release on 2026 Open Call](https://creative-capital.org/press/creative-capital-announces-the-2026-open-call-for-the-creative-capital-award-and-the-launch-of-the-state-of-the-art-prize/)). Joan Mitchell Fellowship deadline (Apr 20, nominated-only — so April is the *nominator's* deadline, not a cold-submit opportunity) ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/joan-mitchell-fellowship)). Headlands AIR portal opens Apr 1 for the following-year cohort ([Headlands Artist in Residence](https://www.headlands.org/program/air/)). National Geographic Level 2 cycle has an April deadline ([NGS Grants and Investments](https://www.nationalgeographic.org/society/grants-and-investments/)).

**May.** Headlands AIR window active (Apr 1 – Jun 1). National Geographic Climate Pledge deadline (late May in 2026) ([NGS Climate Pledge 2026 RFP](https://opportunitydesk.org/2026/04/08/national-geographic-society-rfp-the-climate-pledge-2026/)). Guggenheim application portal opens "late summer" — not yet open. Pollock-Krasner rolling window always active ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/)).

**June.** Headlands AIR deadline Jun 1 ([Headlands AIR page](https://www.headlands.org/program/air/)). Yaddo Summer portal opens Jun 1 (deadline Jul 1) ([Yaddo Apply](https://yaddo.org/apply/)). Vermont Studio Center summer deadline typically late June. Critical Mass portal opens — registration window runs June into early August ([Photolucida Critical Mass](https://www.photolucida.org/critical-mass/)).

**July.** NEA Cycle 2: Grants.gov Part 1 deadline Jul 9, NEA Applicant Portal Part 2 deadline Jul 21 ([NEA 2026 opportunities press release](https://www.arts.gov/news/press-releases/2024/national-endowment-arts-fy-2026-grant-opportunities-now-available)). Yaddo Summer deadline Jul 1 ([Yaddo Apply](https://yaddo.org/apply/)). Ucross Spring deadline Jul 15 for Feb–Jun residencies ([Ucross Residency Program](https://www.ucrossfoundation.org/residency-program.html)). Critical Mass open registration window peaks.

**August.** Critical Mass / Photolucida deadline (historically early-to-mid August; 2025 was extended to Jul 8 so monitor the current year's page directly) ([Photolucida Critical Mass Entry Details](https://www.photolucida.org/critical-mass/entry-details/)). MacDowell Spring/Summer cycle opens Aug 15 ([MacDowell Apply page](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/apply-for-fellowship)). Guggenheim portal opens "late summer" for the following year's Fellows.

**September.** Guggenheim Fellowship first-phase deadline Sep 16/17 ([Guggenheim Application Guidelines](https://www.gf.org/program/how-to-apply)). MacDowell Spring/Summer deadline Sep 10 ([MacDowell Application Guidelines](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/application-guidelines)). **October.** Guggenheim work-samples deadline for invited applicants (~Nov 21; October is prep month). National Geographic Level 2 third cycle typically closes in October. Pollock-Krasner rolling. **December.** Creative Capital biennial open call opens (typically December for Visual Arts, window runs into early April). Yaddo Winter deadline Dec 20 ([Yaddo Apply](https://yaddo.org/apply/)). NEA Cycle 1 of next FY already announced; internal work begins.

## 2. Synthesis block — "what's in the next 30 / 60 / 90 days"

The Orchestrator's ranking narrative should lead with this block when composing a dossier. Bracket the current date and compute the forward windows; do not rank programs whose deadlines fell ≥14 days before the current date unless they run in-year-again.

**Next 30 days.** High-urgency. If any flagship deadline (NEA, Guggenheim, MacDowell, Creative Capital, Yaddo, Critical Mass) falls within 30 days, it outranks any "plan ahead" opportunity regardless of composite score, because prep cost dominates in that window. Dossier copy: "Act now — [program] closes [date]."

**Next 60 days.** Planning range. Residencies requiring 8–10 work samples plus a 1,000-word project statement (MacDowell, Yaddo, Ucross) are workable here if the applicant has a current body. Dossier copy: "Prep block: [program], deadline [date]."

**Next 90 days.** Strategy range. Begin commissioning new writing, pulling references, soliciting letters of support for the Guggenheim (three support letters, each from a specialist in the applicant's field). Nomination-only programs (Joan Mitchell, USA Fellows, Anonymous Was A Woman) require back-channel work — identify potential nominators this window ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/joan-mitchell-fellowship); [United States Artists Fellows](https://www.unitedstatesartists.org/)).

## 3. Seasonal pattern — what the clusters mean

Three clusters dominate the year:

- **February / September.** Fellowship peak. NEA, MacDowell, Guggenheim, Joan Mitchell all cluster applications here. Panels read in spring and fall; results are returned 4–8 months later. A portfolio strong enough for Feb is strong enough for Sep; time shifts the *rubric* (new juror rotation) but not the applicant's baseline readiness.
- **April / July.** Residency peak. MacDowell, Ucross, Yaddo, Headlands, Vermont Studio Center all have April–July windows. Applicants with an active project benefit from this cluster; applicants between projects should defer.
- **August.** Photography-specific peak. Critical Mass is the year's single largest bottleneck for photographers aiming at institutional visibility ([Photolucida Critical Mass FAQ](https://www.photolucida.org/critical-mass/faq/)). If a portfolio is photography-forward, this deadline is non-optional.

Pollock-Krasner is the year's only true rolling window and should be recommended whenever financial-need or emergency-project framing applies ([Pollock-Krasner Grant for Artists](https://www.pkf.org/grants/grant-for-artists/)).

Do not quote historical deadlines as current. Each cited date must be re-verified against the program's application page before the dossier goes out; programs shift windows by 1–3 weeks between years, and 2025's Critical Mass extension to Jul 8 is the kind of quiet shift that breaks a calendar if not re-checked.
