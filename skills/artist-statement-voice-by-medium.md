# artist-statement-voice-by-medium

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Package Drafter alongside `artist-statement-voice.md` when generating `artist_statement` text. This file specifies the lineage references, vocabulary, and pitfalls that vary by medium. The general voice rules in `artist-statement-voice.md` apply universally; this file selects the named-precedent bank and the medium-specific vocabulary. The Drafter reads `akb.practice.primary_medium` and loads the matching section: photography, painting, sculpture, video / new-media, or installation. If the AKB lists multiple primary media, load both sections and let the practice-specific language dominate.

---

## 1. Photography

**Use this section for:** any applicant whose primary medium is photography, including hybrid practices (photo-sculpture, photo-installation, photo-video) where the image is load-bearing.

**Canonical precedents to cite when adjacent.** The Drafter may name at most two precedents per paragraph, never more, and only when the adjacency is load-bearing — not as flattery:

- **Documentary / social-landscape lineage:** Robert Frank (*The Americans*, 1958); Walker Evans (FSA work, 1935–38); Stephen Shore (*Uncommon Places*, 1973–79); Joel Sternfeld (*American Prospects*, 1987); Alec Soth (*Sleeping by the Mississippi*, 2004). Cite when the work sits in the driving-through-America serial register.
- **Deadpan / topographic lineage:** Bernd & Hilla Becher (Typologies, 1960s–2000s, [Tate](https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artists/bernd-becher-and-hilla-becher-718/who-are-bechers)); Lewis Baltz (*The New Industrial Parks near Irvine, California*, 1973–75); the Düsseldorf School (Gursky, Ruff, Höfer, Struth). Cite when the frame is frontal, overcast, serial.
- **Color-landscape / constructed-landscape:** William Eggleston (*William Eggleston's Guide*, MoMA, 1976); Richard Misrach (*Desert Cantos*, 1979–ongoing); Joel Sternfeld. Cite when color is subject, not decoration.
- **Conceptual / durational:** Hiroshi Sugimoto (*Seascapes*, 1980–ongoing, [Fraenkel Gallery](https://fraenkelgallery.com/portfolios/hiroshi-sugimoto-seascapes)); Nicholas Nixon (*The Brown Sisters*, 1975–ongoing). Cite when return-visit structure or extended exposure is load-bearing.
- **Process-forward / material-indexing:** Sally Mann (*Deep South*, 1998–2005, wet-plate collodion, [Getty](https://www.getty.edu/art/exhibitions/sally_mann/inner.html)); Chris McCaw (*Sunburn*, 2003–ongoing, [SFO Museum](https://www.sfomuseum.org/exhibitions/chris-mccaw-sunburn)); Matthew Brandt (Lakes and Reservoirs, [Yossi Milo](https://yossimilo.com/artists/46-matthew-brandt/biography/)); Cheryle St. Onge (*Calling the Birds Home*, 2020–22, [L'Artiere](https://www.lartiere.com/en/prodotto/calling-the-birds-home-cheryle-st-onge/)).
- **Portrait / the body:** Diane Arbus; Peter Hujar (b&w portraits, 1970s–80s, [Pace](https://www.pacegallery.com/artists/peter-hujar/)); Sally Mann (*Immediate Family*); Rineke Dijkstra. Cite when intimacy or directness of address is load-bearing.
- **Re-staging / canonical critique:** Tarrah Krajnak (*Master Rituals II: Weston's Nudes*, 2022, [1854 Photography](https://www.1854.photography/2024/03/tarrah-krajnaks-marginal-meandering-histories/)); Sherrie Levine (*After Walker Evans*, 1981).

**Vocabulary to use.** Named processes: wet-plate collodion, cyanotype, gum bichromate, platinum-palladium, gelatin silver, C-type, dye-transfer, archival pigment. Named papers: Hahnemühle Photo Rag, Arches Platine, Ilford Galerie, Canson Baryta. Named formats: 4x5, 8x10, 35mm, 6x7, 4x5 paper negative. Named vantage / light terms from `aesthetic-vocabulary.md` §2–§3.

**Vocabulary to avoid.** *Capture* (weak, commercial-register), *snap*, *click*, *beautiful light*, *shoot* (context-dependent — acceptable as a verb, disqualifying as a noun meaning a session), *amazing, breathtaking, stunning, dramatic*. Also avoid *painterly* for photographs — it imports a different medium's register and flags a pictorialist-leaning reader.

**Pitfalls specific to photography statements.**
- Front-loading equipment. An 8x10 Deardorff belongs in method, not in the thesis. "My 8x10 camera..." is a weak opener.
- Naming the process without naming what the process *does*. Wet-plate is not self-justifying. "The wet-plate process produces edge failures and chemical drips that are retained as subject matter" is load-bearing; "I use wet-plate collodion" is not.
- Over-citing Ansel Adams. Adams is widely read by institutional panels as the conservative / closed reference point for American landscape — cite only when the work is in direct argument with that tradition, never as blanket lineage.

## 2. Painting

**Canonical precedents to cite when adjacent.**

- **Post-war modernism carrying into contemporary:** Brice Marden (encaustic on canvas, later calligraphic oil); Agnes Martin (grid paintings, 1960s–2000s); Ad Reinhardt (black paintings).
- **Figurative contemporary:** Luc Tuymans (one painting per session, conceived in advance, [Apollo Magazine interview](https://apollo-magazine.com/a-necessary-realism-interview-with-luc-tuymans/)); Marlene Dumas (figure / photograph-derived); Peter Doig (landscape / memory); Mamma Andersson; Dana Schutz; Jenny Saville (figure, large-scale); Kerry James Marshall.
- **Color field / late minimalist extension:** Mary Heilmann; Sean Scully; Charline von Heyl.
- **Photo-derived painting:** Gerhard Richter (blur / photograph); Vija Celmins (graphite, ocean, sky).

**Vocabulary to use.** Named grounds: linen, cotton duck, wood panel, aluminum composite panel. Named media: oil, acrylic, encaustic, oil-stick, flashe, egg tempera. Named procedures: *alla prima*, *glazing*, *scumbling*, *grisaille*, *imprimatura*, *wet-into-wet*, *working lean-to-fat*. See `aesthetic-vocabulary.md` §7 for the full formal-vocabulary list. Named dimensions in inches (US) or centimeters (European / international opportunity).

**Vocabulary to avoid.** *Stunning colors, vibrant palette, dynamic composition, expressive brushstrokes, emotional painting* — all sub-workshop. *Painterly* is occasionally justified in painting statements but is usually a cover for imprecision.

**Pitfalls.**
- Thesis-as-feeling. "These paintings are about grief" is a weak thesis; "These paintings translate the photograph of a Belgian collaborator's trial into a single session of alla-prima grey-ground oil" is load-bearing.
- Over-personal origin stories. Origin belongs in the statement only if the origin is the subject (Tuymans's Flemish post-war history drives his subject choice; naming it is load-bearing).
- Failing to declare scale. Painting scale is a decision jurors read instantly from work samples; name it in the statement rather than forcing the juror to read it from an installation view.

## 3. Sculpture

**Canonical precedents.**

- **Truth-to-materials lineage:** Constantin Brâncuși; Henry Moore; Barbara Hepworth.
- **Post-minimalist / installation-adjacent:** Eva Hesse (latex, fiberglass, rope, 1968–70); Richard Serra (weathering steel, architectural scale); Rachel Whiteread (casts, negative space).
- **Assemblage / accumulation:** Louise Nevelson; Arman; Robert Rauschenberg (*Combines*, 1954–64).
- **Craft-forward / contemporary material practice:** Ursula von Rydingsvard (cedar, graphite, bronze casts); Sheila Hicks (fiber); Theaster Gates (clay and salvaged building materials); Simone Leigh (ceramic and bronze figures).
- **Conceptual / social-practice sculpture:** Mark Dion (cabinet-of-curiosities installations); Tacita Dean (object / film hybrid).

**Vocabulary to use.** Named materials: Corten steel, cold-rolled steel, cast bronze, patinated brass, carrara marble, jesmonite, ciment fondu, raku-fired stoneware, green-sand-cast iron. Named procedures: lost-wax, direct-welded, fabricated, slip-cast, hand-built, press-molded. Named finishes / patinas: liver-of-sulphur, ferric nitrate, cupric nitrate, wax-sealed, unpainted weathering. Named scales from `aesthetic-vocabulary.md` §7: hand / body / architectural.

**Vocabulary to avoid.** *Organic forms, flowing lines, dynamic shapes, powerful presence* — sub-workshop. *Monumental* is acceptable only when literally architectural-scale (Serra, Heizer).

**Pitfalls.**
- Missing dimensions and weight in the statement. Sculptors undersell the physical fact of their work. "20 x 14 x 9 inches, 47 lbs, patinated bronze on a steel rod base" is not tedious; it's load-bearing.
- Failing to name pedestal / mounting logic. Whether the work sits on a plinth (Brâncuși), refuses the plinth (Serra), or hangs from the wall is a decision that affects how the juror reads the image.

## 4. Video / new-media / time-based

**Canonical precedents.**

- **Single-channel video art:** Bill Viola (slow-motion emotional durations); Gary Hill (language, body, video-image); Stan Douglas (looped cinema-register video); Arthur Jafa (*Love is the Message, the Message is Death*, 2016).
- **Multi-channel / installation:** Doug Aitken; Pipilotti Rist (saturated color, projected installation); Stan Douglas (multi-channel).
- **Found-footage / essay:** Harun Farocki; Hito Steyerl (essay video, critical-theory register); Arthur Jafa.
- **Documentary-crossover:** Kahlil Joseph; John Akomfrah (*Vertigo Sea*, 2015; *The Unfinished Conversation*, 2012).
- **Performance-for-video:** Joan Jonas; Tony Oursler; Wu Tsang.

**Vocabulary to use.** Named formats and specifications: single-channel HD 1920x1080 16:9, 24fps, stereo audio, TRT [exact minutes:seconds]; multi-channel (state the channel count and sync method); 4K UHD where the work is shot native. Named delivery media: HDCAM, ProRes 422 HQ, DCP for cinema exhibition. Named equipment: Arri, RED, or the specific camera body only if load-bearing. Named exhibition format: projected vs flat-screen vs monitor-in-plinth — each is a register decision.

Standards reference: the College Art Association's [Guidelines for Presenting Works in Digital Format](https://www.collegeart.org/standards-and-guidelines/guidelines/digital-format) governs the technical specs most institutional applications expect. The Electronic Arts Intermix [Resource Guide](https://www.eai.org/resourceguide/exhibition/singlechannel/basicquestions.html) covers exhibition-specific playback requirements. Cite these when the work depends on specific playback conditions.

**Vocabulary to avoid.** *Captivating, immersive, hypnotic, mesmerizing* — sub-workshop. *Cinematic* is ambiguous and overused; name the specific cinematic register (slow-pan, held-frame, hand-held, fixed-tripod) instead.

**Pitfalls.**
- Omitting duration. TRT is the first fact the juror needs.
- Omitting audio / silent declaration. "Single-channel video, 14:22, stereo audio" or "single-channel video, 14:22, silent" is the required form.
- Describing the narrative instead of the work's construction. Video statements frequently drift into plot summary. The statement describes the work's formal construction and register; the video itself carries narrative.

## 5. Installation

**Canonical precedents.**

- **Site-specific large-scale:** Richard Serra (*Tilted Arc*, 1981; *Torqued Ellipses*, 1996–99); Walter De Maria (*The Lightning Field*, 1977); Michael Heizer; James Turrell (*Roden Crater*, ongoing).
- **Object-dense installation:** Sarah Sze (gravity-defying accumulation); Ann Hamilton (textile, sound, performance).
- **Social / participatory:** Theaster Gates (*Dorchester Projects*, 2009–ongoing); Rick Lowe (*Project Row Houses*); Suzanne Lacy.
- **Mixed-reality / projection:** Doug Aitken; Pipilotti Rist.

**Vocabulary to use.** Named site specifications: footprint (length x width), ceiling height required, wall type (gyprock vs plaster vs unfinished), floor-load capacity (lbs/sqft), electrical (120V standard outlets required, count; or 240V for specific equipment), ambient light condition, noise isolation required, audience flow / dwell-time expectation.

Source: the Art Institute of Chicago's [Staging Site-Specific Installation Art in a Museum Context](https://www.artic.edu/digital-publications/38/perspectives-on-place/37/staging-site-specific-installation-art-in-a-museum-context) covers what museum registrars expect in an installation plan. The [Public Art Installation Proposal Form](https://wpforms.com/templates/public-art-installation-proposal-form-template/) template names the standard site-spec taxonomy.

**Vocabulary to avoid.** *Immersive, transformative, experiential, transcendent* — sub-workshop. *Environment* as a noun is fine when referring to the architectural environment; not fine when used to mean "mood."

**Pitfalls.**
- Missing install-time and de-install-time. Museum and residency installers ask these first; absence reads unprofessional.
- Failing to declare whether the work is re-installable. One-time-only installations require different application framing (they affect archive strategy, photographic documentation as sole record, etc.) than re-installable ones.
- Missing maintenance / conservation note. Institutional acquirers want to know who turns the projector on in 20 years, whether the plant is watered, whether the fabric is replaced.
