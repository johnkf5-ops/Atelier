# cv-format-by-institution

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Package Drafter system prompt for the `cv` material. Supplies the per-institution CV / résumé / career-narrative conventions for the five programs that cover ~70% of mid-career visual-artist applications: Guggenheim, MacDowell, NEA Grants for Arts Projects (Visual Arts), Creative Capital, and Pollock-Krasner. Also covers a generic fallback for foundations without published formatting requirements. Each entry specifies: document name (CV vs résumé vs narrative), section order, date formatting, length cap, explicit include / exclude items, and a one-line rationale for why the program asks for what it asks for. The drafter selects the entry matching `opp.org.name` + `opp.award.type`, and defaults to the generic fallback if no match.

---

## 1. Guggenheim Fellowship

**Document asked for:** *Career Narrative* (not a CV). The Foundation is explicit: "do not substitute a résumé for the career narrative" ([Guggenheim FAQ](https://www.gf.org/faq); [Guggenheim Application Guide 2026](https://competition.gf.org/assets/Guide-to-the-JSGMF-2026.pdf)). A separate *List of Works* (up to 10 items for visual and performance artists) is submitted alongside.

**Career narrative format.**
- Prose, third person, PDF. Suggested length: 1,000–1,500 words (the Foundation does not publish a hard cap for the narrative; the Statement of Plans *is* capped at 1,000 words).
- Lead with a one-paragraph summary of the practice (medium, subject, current body of work).
- Chronological prose through degrees, fellowships, grants, residencies, solo exhibitions, significant group exhibitions, publications, teaching, and collections — integrated, not listed.
- Name prizes, honors, and grants held; named advisers or collaborators when load-bearing; do not name every group show.
- Close with one sentence locating the applicant in current practice.

**List of works format.** Maximum 10 items; each item: title, year, medium, dimensions (or duration), venue / collection if placed. No images in this document — images are submitted as work samples separately.

**Why it reads this way.** The Foundation selects mid-career "well-established" applicants ([Guggenheim FAQ](https://www.gf.org/faq)) and uses the narrative to judge sustained practice, not a data dump. A chronological résumé with 60 group-show bullets buries the practice under the list.

## 2. MacDowell

**Document asked for:** CV / résumé, uploaded as PDF via SlideRoom ([MacDowell Application Guidelines](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/application-guidelines); [MacDowell Apply for Fellowship](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/apply-for-fellowship)).

**Length cap.** Not numerically specified; panel guidance and applicant reports converge on **2 pages**, single-sided. The work-sample panel makes the acceptance decision on the work samples (8 images, past 4 years), not the CV — so the CV's job is to confirm "professional standing," not to argue it.

**Section order for visual artists.**
1. Name, city, website, email at the top.
2. Education (most recent first; degree, institution, year; MFA programs included, non-terminal workshops excluded).
3. Selected Solo Exhibitions (most recent first; year, title, venue, city).
4. Selected Group Exhibitions (most recent first; trim to 10–12 most institutionally-relevant; "(curated by NAME)" if the curator is named on the exhibition's press material).
5. Selected Publications / Bibliography (reviews, books, catalogues).
6. Grants, Awards, Fellowships, Residencies (year, name).
7. Collections (institutional, alphabetical; private collections only if named and notable).
8. Teaching / Visiting Artist positions (year, institution).

**Date format.** Four-digit year, no abbreviated forms (write "2024", not "'24"). Year ranges with en-dash: "2022–2024".

**Exclude:** artist-membership organizations, unrelated employment, high-school credentials, open-call juried regional shows without institutional context, awards below $500, CVs for collaborators (the applicant's CV only).

**Why it reads this way.** MacDowell's anonymous panels, three-year rotations, and "sole criterion: artistic excellence" framing ([MacDowell FAQs](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/faqs)) mean the CV is a confirmation document, not a persuasion document. Trim aggressively.

## 3. NEA Grants for Arts Projects (Visual Arts)

**Document asked for:** NEA grants are institutional, not individual — direct NEA grants go to nonprofits, state arts agencies, and eligible organizations that re-grant to individuals. The "CV" the Visual Arts discipline instructions ask for is an *organizational* capacity document plus *Key Personnel* biographies (one-page max per named participant). The individual artist's application to a state-level NEA re-grant is governed by the state arts council's rules, not the NEA's directly ([NEA Grants for Arts Projects](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects); [NEA How to Apply](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects/how-to-apply)).

**Key-personnel bio format (one page per artist).**
- Prose, third person, ~300–400 words.
- Paragraph one: current practice, primary medium, current body of work.
- Paragraph two: career arc — education, 3–4 most significant exhibitions, 2–3 publications, any collections.
- Paragraph three: relevance to the proposed project — why this artist is the one to do this work.

**Organizational five-year history.** The NEA requires five-year arts-programming history for the applicant organization. Artists re-granted by a state agency are not responsible for this themselves, but if the artist is *leading* a fiscally-sponsored project, the fiscal sponsor's five-year history is the load-bearing document, not the artist's CV.

**Why it reads this way.** The NEA's statutory criteria are "artistic excellence and artistic merit" evaluated at project and organizational scale ([NEA Review Criteria](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects/review-criteria)); individual biographies are read as evidence the organization can execute.

## 4. Creative Capital Award

**Document asked for:** The applicant uploads a project description, biography, résumé, and work samples through the Round I application, then (if advanced) a full résumé, itemized budget, timeline, and expanded samples in Round II ([Creative Capital Grant Application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/); [Creative Capital 2027 Handbook](https://creative-capital.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/2027-Creative-Capital-Application-Handbook.pdf)).

**Round I résumé format.**
- 2 pages, PDF.
- Lead with a 1-paragraph bio (150–200 words, third person) above the résumé itself.
- Sections: Education; Solo Exhibitions (recent 5); Group Exhibitions (recent 8); Publications (recent 8); Grants / Awards / Residencies; Teaching (if load-bearing); Collections.

**Round II expanded résumé.** Expand each section by 50%, no more — Round II panel is reading the project, not the biography.

**Include:** previous Creative Capital-adjacent fellowships (MAP Fund, Warhol, Joan Mitchell, USA Fellowship) if held; prior Creative Capital appearances if any (finalist-only counts).
**Exclude:** BFA degrees if MFA or equivalent terminal is present; vanity publications; self-published press.

**Why it reads this way.** Creative Capital scores "artistic innovation" most heavily, then feasibility, then timing ([Creative Capital Grant Application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)). The Round-I résumé is a timing-and-feasibility confirmation; the work sample and project proposal carry innovation.

## 5. Pollock-Krasner Foundation

**Document asked for:** "a current résumé including an exhibition record," submitted as `LastNameResume.docx` (the `.docx` filename convention is explicit in the Foundation's guidelines) ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/)). A separate cover letter states purpose and amount requested.

**Résumé format.**
- 2–3 pages; Pollock-Krasner does not publish a hard cap but the Foundation notes it values "professional exhibition history" ([Pollock-Krasner General FAQ](https://pkf.org/general-faq/)).
- Sections, in order: Education; Solo Exhibitions; Group Exhibitions; Grants / Awards / Residencies; Collections; Publications / Bibliography; Representation (if any).
- Eligibility: painters, sculptors, artists working on paper including printmakers. Photographers are ineligible *unless* the work crosses into printmaking / paper-based practice. Photographer-only CVs get screened out — don't submit.

**Financial-need note.** The cover letter — not the CV — names purpose and amount. Do not carry financial-need language into the CV.

**Why it reads this way.** Pollock-Krasner is a need-based grant for working artists; the Foundation wants evidence of active exhibition activity, not career-peak credentials. Artists actively exhibiting in professional venues (gallery and museum spaces) are preferred ([Pollock-Krasner Grant for Artists](https://www.pkf.org/grants/grant-for-artists/)).

## 6. Joan Mitchell Fellowship

**Document asked for:** A résumé / CV and a 150-word biography, both uploaded through Submittable, as part of the 33-question application ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship Application Guidelines 2025](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/artist-programs/joan-mitchell-fellowship-application-guidelines-2025)).

**Eligibility.** US-based, primary practice in painting or sculpture, not enrolled in an arts-degree program, nominated by an anonymous nominator. Applicants cannot self-nominate.

**CV format.** 2 pages; standard chronological order (Education, Solo Exhibitions, Group Exhibitions, Publications, Grants/Awards, Collections). 150-word bio separate; bio is first-person-optional (follow the Submittable field's framing).

## 7. Generic fallback (any foundation without a published CV format)

When the opportunity's application page does not specify format, default to:

```
NAME (top, bold, 14pt)
b. YEAR, BIRTHPLACE | Lives and works in CITY, STATE
email | website

EDUCATION
YYYY  Degree, Institution, City

SOLO EXHIBITIONS
YYYY  Title, Venue, City

GROUP EXHIBITIONS (selected)
YYYY  Title, Venue, City (curated by NAME if notable)

PUBLICATIONS (selected)
YYYY  Author, "Title," Publication, pages or URL

GRANTS, AWARDS, RESIDENCIES
YYYY  Name of award, Granting institution

COLLECTIONS
Institution, City (alphabetical)

REPRESENTATION (if any)
Gallery, City (since YYYY)

TEACHING (if load-bearing)
YYYY-YYYY  Position, Institution
```

**Length cap for fallback:** 2 pages. Date format: four-digit year, en-dash ranges. "Selected" preface is load-bearing — it signals the list is curated, not exhaustive, and permits the artist to omit weaker items without looking spotty.

---

## 8. Three cross-cutting rules

1. **Never pad.** A 3-page CV with 50 group shows from regional camera clubs reads *worse* than a tight 1.5-page CV with 8 institutional shows. Trim before sending.
2. **Date-formatting consistency.** Pick four-digit year and en-dash ranges ("2022–2024") and hold that format across all sections. Two-digit years ("'24") read informal; mixing formats reads careless.
3. **"Selected" is a curation claim, not a dodge.** If a section is labeled "Selected Group Exhibitions" it should contain the strongest 8–12, not 35. Panelists who want the full list will ask.
