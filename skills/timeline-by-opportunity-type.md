# timeline-by-opportunity-type

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Orchestrator and Dossier writer when the ranking narrative needs to answer "if I apply now, when do I hear, and when do I start?" Deadline is not the signal that matters; *lead time from deadline to first dollar / first residency day* is. A program with a February deadline and an October notification is structurally different from one with a February deadline and April notification, and the dossier copy must distinguish the two. The rule: match applicant planning horizon to the opportunity's post-deadline clock, not to the deadline itself.

---

## 1. NEA Grants for Arts Projects — ~8 months deadline → notification

**The clock.** Two cycles per fiscal year. Cycle 1 Part-1 deadline mid-February, announcement the following November; Cycle 2 Part-1 deadline mid-July, announcement the following April ([NEA Grants for Arts Projects](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects); [NEA Application Calendar](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects/application-calendar)). Project start dates must fall in the fiscal year following notification; applicants cannot begin the funded activity before the listed start date.

**Implication for the ranking narrative.** NEA is a "plan-a-year-out" opportunity. Do not rank NEA as urgent-in-30-days unless the Part-1 Grants.gov deadline is imminent; NEA requires an SAM.gov registration and UEI number, a 1:1 match, and a nonprofit fiscal sponsor for individual artists (the pathway is available only via specific fellowship programs, not Grants for Arts Projects). Dossier copy should flag the match-funding step explicitly.

**First dollar.** ~10–12 months after Part-1 deadline.

---

## 2. MacDowell Fellowship — ~2.5 months to notification, 6–18 months to residency start

**The clock.** Applicants are notified of admission status approximately 2.5 months after each deadline ([MacDowell FAQs](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/faqs)). Accepted Fellows self-schedule within the residency season the cycle governs: the Feb 10 deadline admits to Fall/Winter (Sep 1 – Feb 28 of the following year), the Sep 10 deadline admits to Spring/Summer (Mar 1 – Aug 31 of the following year) ([MacDowell Application Guidelines](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/application-guidelines)).

**Implication.** A Feb 10 submission means a late-April notification and a first possible residency start in September — seven months from deadline. A Sep 10 submission means a late-November notification and a first possible start in March — six months from deadline. Residencies are 2–8 weeks, with 4 weeks as the typical length.

**Dossier framing.** "Decide now whether you can block 4 weeks in [season + 6 months]; if not, defer to the next cycle." MacDowell admits based on work samples from the last four years — a January application benefits from December's shortlist of strongest recent frames.

---

## 3. Yaddo — ~3 months to notification

**The clock.** Two cycles. Summer deadline Jul 1 governs residencies starting November through the following June; Winter deadline Dec 20 governs residencies starting May through the following March ([Yaddo Apply](https://yaddo.org/apply/)). Notification is roughly three months post-deadline; residencies begin as early as four months post-notification.

**Implication.** Yaddo is the shortest deadline-to-residency pipeline among the flagship US residencies. A July application can have a boots-on-ground start by November. The Orchestrator should rank Yaddo higher than MacDowell for applicants with near-term project deadlines (book dummy due, exhibition in fall, etc.) who can use a 4–8 week residency as the final push.

---

## 4. Creative Capital Award — ~5 months total across two rounds

**The clock.** Round 1 open call typically December — early April. Round 2 invited finalists submit full project + budget several months later; final Awards announced the following spring ([Creative Capital 2026 open call press release](https://creative-capital.org/press/creative-capital-announces-the-2026-open-call-for-the-creative-capital-award-and-the-launch-of-the-state-of-the-art-prize/)). Grants of up to $50,000 are unrestricted and project-based; the State of the Art Prize awards $10,000 to one artist in each of 53 states/territories.

**Implication.** Creative Capital is a two-stage commitment: Round 1 is a lightweight "project idea + samples" application, Round 2 is a full budget with timelines. Applicants advance from ~4,500 submissions to ~100 finalists to ~50 awardees. Dossier copy should describe Creative Capital as "biennial for Visual Arts — miss this window and the next is two calendar years out," which dominates the urgency calculus.

**First dollar.** ~8–10 months after Round 1 deadline for successful applicants.

## 5. Guggenheim Fellowship — ~7 months deadline → notification

**The clock.** First-phase application deadline Sep 16/17; invited applicants submit work samples by ~Nov 21; Board of Trustees announces Fellows in mid-April of the following year ([Guggenheim FAQ](https://www.gf.org/faq); [Guggenheim Application Guidelines](https://www.gf.org/program/how-to-apply)). 2024 announcement: 188 Fellows named on April 11.

**Implication.** A September application begins a seven-month silence. Applicants should not submit and then wait; instead the ranking narrative should recommend parallel submissions (MacDowell Sep 10, Yaddo Dec 20) to keep the pipeline producing results during the wait. Fellowship amounts vary by discipline and are unrestricted; the Photography Fellowship is underwritten by the Robert Frank Foundation.

**Prep constraint.** Three letters of recommendation from specialists in the applicant's field, plus a career narrative and a project plan. Back-channel work to line up recommenders should begin ~Aug 1 — a Sep 17 submission with recommenders locked-in on Sep 10 is under-prepared.

## 6. Pollock-Krasner — 6–8 weeks (rolling)

**The clock.** Rolling deadline year-round ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/)). Applicants submit a financial-need statement, career narrative, and work samples. Grants are up to $50,000 and are unrestricted for a one-year period ([Pollock-Krasner Grant for Artists](https://www.pkf.org/grants/grant-for-artists/)).

**Implication.** Pollock-Krasner is the only rolling window at this prestige tier. Dossier copy should recommend it whenever the applicant's AKB indicates financial-need triggers (lost studio, medical, emergency travel for a body of work). Because the turnaround is 6–8 weeks, it functions as a short-term bridge; do not frame it as a career-milestone award.

---

## 7. Critical Mass / Photolucida — ~2–4 months deadline → cohort announcement

**The clock.** Registration historically closes early-to-mid August (in 2025 extended to Jul 8 — re-verify the current year's page) ([Photolucida Critical Mass Entry Details](https://www.photolucida.org/critical-mass/entry-details/)). Top 200 finalists announced mid-August; Top 50 announced late September / early October ([Photolucida Top 50](https://www.photolucida.org/critical-mass/top-50/)). Awards (monograph publication, solo exhibitions) follow into the first quarter of the subsequent year.

**Implication.** A $50+ tiered-entry fee with a 2–4 month turnaround; the "Top 200" listing alone is a citable CV line. Dossier copy should frame Critical Mass as *the* photography-specific deadline of the calendar year — missing it costs 12 months.

## 8. Residency-family summary

| Program | Deadline → notify | Notify → start |
|---|---|---|
| MacDowell | ~2.5 mo | 6–18 mo |
| Yaddo | ~3 mo | 4–11 mo |
| Ucross | ~2.5 mo | 6–12 mo ([Ucross Residency Program](https://www.ucrossfoundation.org/residency-program.html)) |
| Vermont Studio Center | ~2 mo | 6–9 mo ([VSC Apply](https://vermontstudiocenter.org/apply)) |
| Headlands AIR | ~6 mo | 12+ mo ([Headlands AIR](https://www.headlands.org/program/air/)) |

Headlands notifies by Dec 11 for residencies *the following year* — the longest post-notification runway in the set.

---

## 9. What the Drafter uses this for

The per-match dossier copy must contain two explicit numbers: days-to-deadline and months-to-start. The ranking narrative's urgency framing ("needs prep THIS MONTH" vs "plan ahead for Q3") draws from this file. A program with a deadline inside 30 days and notification inside 3 months is "act now." A program with a deadline inside 30 days and notification inside 8 months is "submit and forget" — the dossier should then propose an additional near-term opportunity whose notification arrives first, to keep the applicant's morale and pipeline alive during the silence.
