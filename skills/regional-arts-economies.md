# regional-arts-economies

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Orchestrator when the AKB's `identity.home_base.state` or `home_base.city` maps to a named region, and into the Dossier writer when the ranking narrative should lean into local programs before national ones. State arts-council fellowships, regional foundation grants, and geography-bounded residencies form a parallel pipeline that Orchestrator runs routinely miss because their search defaults to national flagships. Regional signals are *less* prestigious in absolute terms but have dramatically higher acceptance rates for resident applicants and often produce the CV lines that national flagships need as eligibility prerequisites. This file is the per-state index. Cite each program's application page when the narrative names it.

---

## California

California's individual-artist pipeline runs through the California Arts Council's IAF program, regional residencies, and Bay Area / LA gallery infrastructure.

- **California Arts Council Individual Artist Fellowship.** Three-tier unrestricted grant: Emerging $5,000, Established $10,000, Legacy $50,000; administered regionally through eight designated Administering Organizations ([California Arts Council IAF](https://arts.ca.gov/grant_program/individual-artists-fellowship/); [California Grants Portal](https://www.grants.gov/search-results-detail/ca-individual-artist-fellowship/)). Not project-based — unrestricted use. Applicants must apply through the AO serving their region.
- **Headlands Center for the Arts (Sausalito).** Artist-in-Residence program with June 1 annual deadline, fully sponsored 4–10 week residencies ([Headlands AIR](https://www.headlands.org/program/air/)). A top-three US residency signal; McLaughlin Foundation Award at Headlands awards an additional $10,000 to an established visual artist.
- **SFMOMA Artists Gallery** (historically in Fort Mason) and the **Pier 24 Photography** programming cluster anchor the Bay Area photography infrastructure.
- **LA Louver** runs periodic open-submission windows for its Rogue Wave group-exhibition series of LA-region emerging artists ([LA Louver Rogue Wave](https://www.lalouver.com/)).
- **Creative Capital** is headquartered in New York but the Open Call is national; Californians often cluster well.

Dossier framing: "In-state pathway is California Arts Council → Headlands → Creative Capital → national flagship." Regional fellowship first, residency second, national grant third.

## New York

New York's pipeline is the densest in the US. Resident applicants should saturate regional infrastructure before touching national programs.

- **NYSCA (New York State Council on the Arts).** Support via regranting partners including NYFA, A.R.T./New York, etc. ([NYSCA](https://arts.ny.gov/)). Requires NY residency plus fiscal sponsor for individual artists in most programs.
- **NYFA Source.** Aggregator of artist opportunities plus the NYFA Fiscal Sponsorship program — the most commonly used individual-artist fiscal sponsor in the US ([NYFA](https://www.nyfa.org/)).
- **Joan Mitchell Foundation.** Based Brooklyn; runs the Joan Mitchell Fellowship (nomination-only, $60,000 over 5 years) and the Joan Mitchell Artist Residency in New Orleans ([Joan Mitchell Foundation](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/)).
- **Artists Space** (SoHo) — exhibition and programming; open-call opportunities periodic ([Artists Space](https://artistsspace.org/)).
- **Lower Manhattan Cultural Council (LMCC).** Workspace residencies (Governors Island, Arts Center at Governors Island), Creative Engagement grant ([LMCC](https://lmcc.net/)).
- **MacDowell.** NH-based but runs an NYC Open House and has the densest NYC fellow alumni network ([MacDowell Apply](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/apply-for-fellowship)).

Dossier framing: "NYSCA via fiscal sponsor → LMCC → Joan Mitchell Fellowship (if nominated) → Guggenheim."

## Texas

- **Texas Commission on the Arts (TCA).** Arts Respond Project grants; eligibility typically via nonprofit fiscal sponsor ([TCA](https://www.arts.texas.gov/)).
- **Artpace (San Antonio).** International Artist-in-Residence program: twice a year, three residencies per cycle (one international, one national, one Texas-based); $6,000 stipend, $10,000 production budget, studio, apartment, 2-month residency, final exhibition. Unsolicited applications are not considered; Texas-based artists apply to the rolling Texas Open Call statewide database, from which guest curators select ([Artpace International AIR](https://artpace.org/about/international-artist-in-residence/); [Texas Open Call](https://artpace.org/about/international-artist-in-residence/texas-open-call/)).
- **Linda Pace Foundation (San Antonio).** Ruby City exhibition programming; not a granting body but an important collecting institution.
- **Glassell School of Art at MFAH (Houston).** Core Residency — two-year critical-studies residency for emerging artists ([Glassell Core](https://www.mfah.org/programs/glassell-core-program)).
- **Lawndale Art Center (Houston).** Artist Studio Program, annual Big Show exhibition ([Lawndale Art Center](https://www.lawndaleartcenter.org/)).
- **Ucross (Wyoming).** Close enough for regional pitch from Texas if the applicant's narrative frames it so; Ucross is not Texas-bounded but its high-plains programming attracts Texas-based land and landscape artists ([Ucross](https://www.ucrossfoundation.org/residency-program.html)).

Dossier framing: "Texas Open Call registration (year-round) → Lawndale Big Show → Core Residency or Artpace → national flagship."

## Minnesota

Minnesota punches well above state population in individual-artist funding — relevant to John's bi-coastal MN/NV positioning.

- **McKnight Visual Artist Fellowships.** Administered by MCAD since 1981. Six $25,000 fellowships per year across painting, sculpture, drawing, photography, printmaking, mixed media, installation, multimedia, film, video, sound. Requires MN residency for at least one year prior plus 8+ years of solo/group exhibition, residency, or publication experience ([McKnight Visual Artist Fellowships](https://www.mcad.edu/why-mcad/fellowships/mcknight-visual-artist-fellowships)). Mid-to-late-career target.
- **Jerome Hill Artist Fellowship.** Multi-year fellowship administered by the Jerome Foundation for early-career generative artists in MN and NYC ([Jerome Foundation](https://www.jeromefdn.org/)). $50,000 over two years.
- **Minnesota State Arts Board (MSAB).** Artist Initiative Grant and Creative Individuals grants ([MSAB](https://www.arts.state.mn.us/)).
- **Weisman Art Museum (U of MN).** Collecting institution; no open-call but local artist inclusion in programming.
- **MCAD residencies.** Sandbox residencies, studio spaces, and the McKnight Artists' Resources (MARs) professional-development programming ([MCAD](https://www.mcad.edu/)).

Dossier framing for MN residents: "MSAB Artist Initiative → McKnight Visual Artist Fellowship → Jerome → national flagship (NEA, Guggenheim)."

## Nevada

Nevada is a small pond with unusually strong signal for Nevada residents because the state's artist-fellowship pool is small and the fellowship's national recognition is growing. Relevant to John's Las Vegas base.

- **Nevada Arts Council Artist Fellowship.** $5,000 unrestricted, no match required; FY27 deadline May 1, 2026; concealed-review process ([Nevada Arts Council Artist Services](https://www.nvartscouncil.org/programs/artist-services/)). Eligibility: individual artist, NV resident, 21+, non-student; capped at two prior fellowships. Announced in §3.6 of the Atelier build plan as the opportunity that scored highest for John's profile.
- **Nevada Arts Council Artist Services Grant.** $3,000 project-based grants for production/presentation ([Artist Services](https://www.nvartscouncil.org/programs/artist-services/)).
- **Bellagio Gallery of Fine Art (historical), Nevada Museum of Art (Reno).** NMA is the state's flagship collecting institution; emphasis on art-and-environment programming ([Nevada Museum of Art](https://www.nevadaart.org/)).
- **Stratosphere Gallery and other Las Vegas galleries.** Regional exhibition pipeline; John has gallery representation there.

Dossier framing for NV residents: "Nevada Arts Council Fellowship is *the* top-ranked opportunity when eligible; apply every cycle until the two-fellowship cap is reached."

## Illinois

- **Illinois Arts Council Agency (IACA) Artist Fellowship.** Recognizes exceptional work by IL artists; $20,000 Artist Fellowship awards and $1,500 Finalist awards ([Illinois Arts Council Artist Fellowship](https://arts.illinois.gov/)).
- **3Arts (Chicago).** Awards unrestricted $30,000 cash prizes to Chicago women artists, artists of color, and deaf and disabled artists ([3Arts](https://3arts.org/)).
- **MCA Chicago** (collecting + exhibition), **Columbia College Chicago** (photography programs), **Rhona Hoffman Gallery** anchor Chicago's contemporary photography infrastructure.

## New Mexico

- **New Mexico Arts.** Art in Public Places program and individual-artist grants ([New Mexico Arts](https://www.newmexicoculture.org/)).
- **Santa Fe Art Institute (SFAI).** Thematic annual residency (theme cycles annually — e.g., "Truth," "Labor") ([SFAI](https://sfai.org/)).
- **Lannan Foundation.** Residencies and literary awards ([Lannan](https://www.lannan.org/)).
- **Bosque Center / 516 ARTS (Albuquerque)** for emerging-artist exhibition.

## Other notable regional programs

- **Massachusetts.** Mass Cultural Council Artist Fellowships ($15,000, cycles per discipline) ([Mass Cultural Council](https://massculturalcouncil.org/)).
- **Oregon.** Oregon Arts Commission Individual Artist Fellowships; Photolucida (Portland) is the Critical Mass home.
- **Maine.** Maine Arts Commission; Dowling Walsh, Cove Street Arts, Haystack Mountain School of Crafts.
- **Wyoming.** Ucross; Brush Creek Foundation for the Arts residencies.

## How the Orchestrator uses this file

When the AKB identifies a home-base state, the Orchestrator must (a) rank that state's top individual-artist fellowship above comparable national programs in the next 90 days, (b) add the state's top residency to the ranked list if a deadline falls in the window, and (c) flag regional-pipeline programs (galleries, museum residencies) in the "narrative" column even when they aren't in the current deadline window, so the Dossier writer can reference them as context. The regional programs produce the CV lines that national flagships require as eligibility prerequisites; treating them as lower-priority is a common Orchestrator mis-ranking.
