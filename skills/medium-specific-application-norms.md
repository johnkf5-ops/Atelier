# medium-specific-application-norms

WHEN TO USE: Loaded into the Package Drafter for the `project_proposal` and `work_sample_selection` materials whenever the application requires specification of technical / material details about the work. Different media carry different hard requirements: photographers must supply print specifications and edition data; sculptors must supply dimensions, weight, and materials; painters must supply substrate and ground; video artists must supply duration and playback format; installation artists must supply site requirements. This file codifies the required fields per medium, the standard formatting, and the specific institutions that enforce each requirement. The Drafter reads `akb.practice.primary_medium` and loads the matching section; where primary medium is hybrid, load both.

---

## 1. Photography

**Required fields per work (institutional minimum).**

- **Title, year** — year the negative / file was made, not the year of print production. If print date differs, note as "printed 2024" after the dimensions.
- **Medium / process** — gelatin silver, archival pigment, platinum-palladium, dye-transfer, C-type, chromogenic, ambrotype, tintype, cyanotype, gum bichromate. For digital work, "archival pigment print" is the institutional-standard phrase ([SOHN Fine Art: Archival Pigment Printing](https://www.sohnfineart.com/archival-pigment-printing)).
- **Paper / substrate** — Hahnemühle Photo Rag, Arches Platine, Ilford Galerie Prestige, Canson Baryta, Museo Silver Rag. Name the specific paper when load-bearing to the print's register; "archival matte cotton rag" is acceptable if the specific paper varies across the edition.
- **Print size** — height x width in inches or centimeters. Image size and paper size are both relevant; if they differ, list as "image 30 x 40 in, paper 32 x 42 in."
- **Edition** — state edition size + number of artist proofs. Standard institutional practice: "edition of 5 + 2 AP" or "edition of 7 + 1 AP" for exhibition-scale prints; "edition of 25 + 3 AP" for book-scale ([Tony Newlin Gallery: Pigment Prints](https://www.tonynewlin.com/pigment-prints)). Unique pieces ("1/1" or "unique") must be marked.
- **Mount / presentation** — framed / unframed, frame type (walnut, maple, aluminum, painted), glazing (museum glass, UV acrylic, plexiglass, unglazed), float-mounted or dry-mounted, float depth.

**Format conventions.**
- `Title`, Year. Medium, dimensions (height x width), edition. Mount if relevant.
- Example: *Kennebec River at Hallowell, Thaw #3*, 2023. Archival pigment print on Hahnemühle Photo Rag, 40 x 50 in, edition of 5 + 2 AP. Framed in maple with museum glass.

**Institution-specific.**
- **MacDowell:** 8 images from past 4 years; 2 may be detail / installation views; images should be <3 MB, max 5 MB ([MacDowell Work Sample Requirements](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/work-sample-requirements)).
- **Yaddo:** 7 images; optional documentary video of installation ([Yaddo Work Sample Requirements](https://yaddo.org/work-sample-requirements/)).
- **Guggenheim:** Visual artists submit up to 10 items in the *List of Works* — title, year, medium, dimensions, venue / collection. Work samples (images) are separate ([Guggenheim Application Handbook, 2026](https://competition.gf.org/assets/Guide-to-the-JSGMF-2026.pdf)).
- **Creative Capital:** Visual Arts applicants submit up to two image or video samples; images at least 72 dpi ([Creative Capital Grant Application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)).
- **Pollock-Krasner:** 10 digital images with a corresponding identification list; images must be of *current* work ([Pollock-Krasner How to Apply](https://www.pkf.org/apply/how-to-apply/)). Note: Pollock-Krasner's eligibility excludes photographers whose practice is strictly photographic — the Foundation funds painters, sculptors, printmakers, and paper-based artists.

## 2. Painting

**Required fields per work.**

- **Title, year.**
- **Medium** — oil, acrylic, oil and wax, egg tempera, encaustic, casein, flashe (a specific brand of matte vinyl paint), oil-stick, mixed media. Specificity matters: "oil" reads institutional; "paint" reads amateur.
- **Substrate / support** — canvas, linen (Belgian linen if premium), cotton duck, birch panel, aluminum composite panel (ACM), wood panel (species when relevant). Stretched / un-stretched is a register decision — un-stretched linen hung from the top edge reads different from stretched-over-bars.
- **Ground / preparation** — mentioned in proposal or statement when load-bearing ("lead-tin yellow under-ground," "raw linen with rabbit-skin-glue size," "double-primed with gesso"). Omit in image captions.
- **Dimensions** — height x width (x depth if the panel is deep) in inches or centimeters. US applications use inches; European / international use centimeters. Be consistent within an application.

**Format conventions.**
- `Title`, Year. Medium on substrate, dimensions.
- Example: *Afterimage VII*, 2024. Oil on un-stretched linen, 60 x 48 in.

**Institution-specific.**
- **Pollock-Krasner:** painters are a core eligible group; the résumé exhibition record is load-bearing ([Pollock-Krasner Grant for Artists](https://www.pkf.org/grants/grant-for-artists/)).
- **Joan Mitchell Fellowship:** painters are one of two eligible disciplines (painting + sculpture); 10 work samples through Submittable ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship Guidelines](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/artist-programs/joan-mitchell-fellowship-application-guidelines-2025)).
- **NEA Grants for Arts Projects (Visual Arts):** grants are to organizations, not individuals; painters enter via fiscal sponsorship or state-level re-grants ([NEA Grants for Arts Projects](https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects)).

## 3. Sculpture

**Required fields per work.**

- **Title, year.**
- **Materials** — the full material stack, primary-to-secondary: "cast iron, patinated steel rod armature, salvaged oak base." Truth-in-materials is an institutional norm; hiding secondary materials reads poorly.
- **Process** — cast (lost-wax, green-sand, investment); direct-welded; fabricated; hand-built; slip-cast; press-molded; carved (sub-species: carved-stone, carved-wood); assembled.
- **Dimensions** — height x width x depth, in inches or centimeters. Always three axes for sculpture; omitting depth reads as a photograph of a 3D object, not a 3D object itself.
- **Weight** — lbs or kg. Load-bearing for large works (museum registrars ask; shippers ask; installation crews ask). Omit for works under ~10 lbs where the weight is not a constraint.
- **Base / pedestal** — integral (Brâncuși), refused (Serra, floor works), custom-fabricated-by-artist, exhibition-provided. Name the intended display condition.
- **Edition** for cast work — same conventions as photography. Unique works marked "unique" or "1/1."

**Format conventions.**
- `Title`, Year. Materials, dimensions (h x w x d), weight, edition or unique.
- Example: *Kennebec Drift II*, 2023. Cast iron with ferric-nitrate patina, steel rod armature, 22 x 18 x 14 in, 47 lbs, unique.

**Institution-specific.**
- **Joan Mitchell Fellowship:** sculptors alongside painters; both disciplines have parity in the nomination and review process ([Joan Mitchell Fellowship Guidelines](https://www.joanmitchellfoundation.org/artist-programs/joan-mitchell-fellowship-application-guidelines-2025)).
- **Pollock-Krasner:** sculptors eligible; exhibition record expected.
- **MacDowell:** 3D works; 2 of the 8 images may be detail or installation views; one brief documentation video (<2 min) showing installation is allowed, counting as one of the eight ([MacDowell Work Sample Requirements](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/work-sample-requirements)).

## 4. Video / new-media / time-based

**Required fields per work.**

- **Title, year.**
- **Format** — single-channel video; multi-channel video (state channel count); interactive / real-time; generative. If multi-channel, state the sync method (SMPTE timecode, genlock, slaved playback).
- **Resolution** — HD 1920 x 1080, 4K UHD 3840 x 2160, or cinema DCP 2K / 4K. Native resolution, not upscaled.
- **Aspect ratio** — 16:9, 4:3, 1.85:1, 2.39:1, or custom (state).
- **Frame rate** — 24fps for cinematic register; 30 or 60fps for broadcast / sports-derived register; 25fps PAL-region; state native rate.
- **Duration / TRT (total running time)** — minutes:seconds; "looped" if the work loops, with loop length stated.
- **Audio** — stereo / 5.1 / silent. State explicitly; silence is a declaration, not a default.
- **Delivery format** — ProRes 422 HQ, H.264, DCP, or other. State the delivery master format that an exhibition context would receive.
- **Playback / exhibition specification** — projected (state projector specs required: lumens minimum, throw distance); flat-screen (state screen size and model if critical); monitor-in-plinth; tablet / handheld. Reference the CAA standards ([Guidelines for Presenting Works in Digital Format](https://www.collegeart.org/standards-and-guidelines/guidelines/digital-format)) where applicable.

**Format conventions.**
- `Title`, Year. Single-channel video, 1920 x 1080, 16:9, 24fps, stereo, TRT 9:13.
- Example: *Thaw Loop*, 2024. Single-channel HD video, 1920 x 1080, 16:9, 24fps, stereo audio, TRT 9:13, looped (loop length 9:13); installed as projection at 12 ft wide on a matte-white screen.

**Institution-specific.**
- **MacDowell video work:** up to 8 videos, each <2 min, installation image counts as one of eight ([MacDowell Work Sample Requirements](https://www.macdowell.org/apply/work-sample-requirements)).
- **Creative Capital:** video samples allowed in lieu of images; no fixed duration cap but panels review dense material at Round I ([Creative Capital Grant Application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)).
- **Film / video museum exhibition standards:** the Electronic Arts Intermix [Resource Guide](https://www.eai.org/resourceguide/exhibition/singlechannel/basicquestions.html) and [Matters in Media Art](http://mattersinmediaart.org/assessing-time-based-media-art.html) document what major museums expect when acquiring or exhibiting time-based media.

## 5. Installation

**Required fields per work.**

- **Title, year.**
- **Materials** — the full material inventory, separated by function: structural, surface, media equipment, consumables, performance elements.
- **Site footprint** — length x width x ceiling height, in feet or meters. The minimum ceiling height requirement is load-bearing.
- **Weight / floor load** — total weight and lbs/sqft point load for any concentrated-weight element.
- **Electrical requirements** — 120V outlet count, 240V outlet count if required, amperage draw at peak, whether a dedicated circuit is required. Standard museum gallery exhibition spec is 120V 20A circuits.
- **Water / plumbing** — state if required; most institutions cannot accommodate water-based installations without advance coordination.
- **Ambient conditions** — light (full-dark, low-ambient, daylight-acceptable); sound (noise-isolated room required vs shared gallery acceptable); temperature / humidity range.
- **Mounting / hanging** — wall-hung (state hardware and wall type); floor-standing; ceiling-suspended (rigging weight limits); freestanding; plinth-mounted.
- **Audience flow and dwell time** — expected visitors / hour; timed entry required; minimum dwell time.
- **Install / de-install time** — hours or days required, crew size required, specialist trades required (electrician, rigger, AV tech).
- **Maintenance plan** — who turns it on / off, daily checks, consumables replaced on what schedule, year-scale conservation plan.

**Format conventions.** Installation works typically ship to juries as a single PDF with plan-view diagram, elevation, materials list, and site-spec sheet, not as image captions. The Art Institute of Chicago's [Staging Site-Specific Installation Art in a Museum Context](https://www.artic.edu/digital-publications/38/perspectives-on-place/37/staging-site-specific-installation-art-in-a-museum-context) and the [Public Art Installation Proposal Form](https://wpforms.com/templates/public-art-installation-proposal-form-template/) define the standard document set.

**Institution-specific.**
- **MacDowell:** installation documentation video <2 min allowed; installation images (2 of 8) count toward the 8-image cap.
- **Public art commissions:** require the fullest version of this document — plan view, elevation, materials, electrical, fabrication plan, insurance binder, maintenance plan, all assembled in a single response package. Public art panels are strongly risk-averse toward applicants without a prior site-scale installation on the CV.
- **Museum site-specific installation:** add a conservation-strategy note addressing obsolescence of any equipment used (projector bulbs, hard-drive storage, proprietary software).

## 6. Cross-medium universal rules

- **Imperial or metric, not both.** US institutional applications use inches and pounds; European use centimeters and kilograms. Pick one per application and hold it.
- **Title italics and punctuation.** Work titles are italicized in prose; non-italic in data-dense spec sheets. Single quotes around titles reads amateur.
- **Year conventions.** Four-digit year ("2023"), never apostrophe-shorthand ("'23"). Year range: en-dash ("2022–2024"). Ongoing work: "2022–ongoing".
- **Edition transparency.** Edition size, AP count, and any prior-edition history stated flat. Institutional acquirers check editions against registry records; discrepancies disqualify.
- **Provenance and condition.** For any work offered for donation or sale (not for application samples), a one-line condition note and a one-line exhibition history are the institutional minimum — museum registrars read condition first.
